# Etymology Coverage Tracker

This document tracks which etymological routes, language families, and semantic domains are already covered to prevent duplicate journeys.

## Covered Routes (DO NOT DUPLICATE)

### Arabic → Europe (via trade/scholarship)
- qahwa (coffee), algorithm, algebra, alchemy, admiral, zero, monsoon, assassin, magazine*
- **Route**: Baghdad/Arabia → Moorish Spain/Mediterranean → Europe
- **Mechanism**: Islamic Golden Age scholarship, trade

### Sanskrit/Hindi → English (via colonialism)
- saans, daan, karma, avatar, guru, nirvana, jungle, shampoo, pajamas, juggernaut*
- **Route**: India → British colonial administration → English
- **Mechanism**: British Raj, yoga/spiritual movements

### Persian → Europe (via trade/Crusades)
- checkmate, bazaar, paradise
- **Route**: Persia → Arabic → Crusader contact/trade → Europe
- **Mechanism**: Silk Road trade, chess diffusion

### Japanese → Global
- tsunami, karate, origami, manga, tycoon
- **Route**: Japan → direct borrowing in 19th-20th century
- **Mechanism**: Cultural exchange, post-WWII influence

### Chinese → English (via trade)
- ketchup (Hokkien), gung-ho, typhoon (partial)
- **Route**: Southern China ports → Southeast Asia → European traders
- **Mechanism**: Maritime trade routes

### Bantu/African → Global
- ubuntu, safari (via Arabic)
- **Route**: African languages → colonial contact/post-colonial
- **Mechanism**: Colonialism, anti-apartheid movement

### Pacific/Austronesian → English
- tattoo, taboo (Polynesian), boomerang (Dharug)
- **Route**: Pacific islands/Australia → explorers → English
- **Mechanism**: Captain Cook voyages, colonization

### German compounds → English
- wanderlust, kindergarten
- **Route**: Direct borrowing of compound words
- **Mechanism**: Cultural concepts without English equivalent

### Latin classical → English
- Many words (donate, muscle*, salary*)
- **Route**: Latin → French → English OR Latin → scientific terminology
- **Mechanism**: Norman conquest, scientific revolution
- **Note**: This route is extremely common; avoid unless exceptional story

### Nahuatl → Spanish → Global
- chocolate
- **Route**: Aztec Mexico → Spanish conquest → Europe
- **Mechanism**: Columbian exchange

### Slavic → Global
- robot (Czech)
- **Route**: Coined in literature → borrowed whole
- **Mechanism**: Karel Čapek's play R.U.R.

### Old Norse → English
- berserk
- **Route**: Viking contact with Britain
- **Mechanism**: Norse settlement, sagas

## RULES FOR NEW WORDS

1. **Check this document first** - don't duplicate a route
2. **Prefer uncovered routes** - Korean, Hebrew, Yiddish, Quechua, etc.
3. **If using a covered route**, the word must have an exceptional/unique story
4. **Semantic diversity** - don't cluster in same domain
5. **Journey distinctiveness** - different geographic path, different historical mechanism
6. **Modern relevance** - word should still be used today

## WORD IDEAS BY UNCOVERED ROUTE

### Korean
- **Taekwondo**: tae (foot) + kwon (fist) + do (way) - martial art as philosophy
- **Aegyo**: Cute/childish behavior - K-pop cultural export

### Hebrew/Yiddish
- **Golem**: Animated clay figure - Prague legend, AI metaphor today
- **Chutzpah**: Audacity/nerve - untranslatable attitude
- **Mensch**: Person of integrity - Yiddish moral concept

### Quechua
- **Condor**: Kuntur - sacred bird, Andes to English
- **Quinoa**: Kinuwa - ancient grain to superfood

### Tamil/Dravidian
- **Catamaran**: Kattumaram (tied logs) - boat design that went global
- **Pariah**: Paraiyar - caste became metaphor

### Malay
- **Amok**: Running amok - psychological state
- **Orangutan**: Orang (person) + hutan (forest) - "person of the forest"

### Hungarian
- **Coach**: Kocsi (from town of Kocs) - vehicle became verb
- **Paprika**: The spice that defines Hungarian cuisine
